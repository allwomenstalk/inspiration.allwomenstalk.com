# What is Personality ?

**What is personality**? Well, psychologists do not actually agree on a single definition of what constitutes personality, but they generally describe it as being made up of the characteristic patterns of thoughts, feelings, and behaviors that make us unique. According to researchers, some external factors can influence the expression of certain traits and personality tends to remain consistent throughout our lives. Read on for the answer to **what is personality**.

## 1. Personality is Organized and Consistent

Not only does personality remain constant, but it is also organized. We tend to express certain aspects of who we are in different situations and our responses are generally stable. This is one of the best answers for **what is personality**.

## 2. Personality Causes Behavior

We react to the people and objects in our environment based on our personality. From personal preferences to the career we choose, every aspect of our lifestyle is affected by personality.

Sigmund Freud’s psychodynamic theory of personality is one of the most famous and respected. It emphasizes the influence of the unconscious mind and childhood experiences. Freud believed the 3 components of personality were the id, the ego, and the superego. The id is responsible for all needs and urges, while the superego for ideals and morals. The ego moderates between the demands of the id, the superego, and reality.
