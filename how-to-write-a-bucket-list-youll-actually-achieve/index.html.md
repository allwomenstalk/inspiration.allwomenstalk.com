# How to Write a Bucket List Youll Actually Achieve ...

Think about what you really want to do in this life and learn **how to write a bucket list you'll actually achieve**. Embrace all of the places and things life has to offer. The Science of People gives great advice on **how to write a bucket list you'll actually achieve**. Here are 4 steps to follow:

## 1. Think about What You Want

![product, girl, fun, student,](https://img.allw.mn/content/aj/d2/gtm192g559b02d689ea7c644222858_245x133.gif)

The first step Science of People recommends on **how to write a bucket list you'll actually achieve**, is to make a list of all the things you want to do, and not to restrict yourself when making the list. Your list can include big leaps or small adventures, the choice is up to you. Psychology Today suggests thinking of both large and small goals.

## 2. Think of a Timeline

![lighting, girl, darkness, drink,](https://img.allw.mn/content/i3/zh/z1qcnl6059b02dafca09b426432079_500x235.gif)

What is the ideal time in your life to accomplish a goal on your bucket list? Are there small steps you can take now to help you reach a bigger goal later? Is there a certain time of year this task would be best accomplished?

## 3. Make Time to Work on Your Goals

![finger, material, writing,](https://img.allw.mn/content/t8/fa/zwprtfl059b02dbe090a7953217444_694x332.gif)

Set small amounts of time aside to work on the small items on your list, or take a larger amount of time to work on a big goal. Think about how much it costs (time and money), and consider the best options. For example, if your want to travel, find the best available package deals, dates, and ticket prices.

## 4. Go for It!

![yellow, human, girl, tradition, smile,](https://img.allw.mn/content/of/y1/pjfozll159b02e52901a3896144879_500x259.gif)

Book your ticket, climb the mountain, cross the item off your list, and move on to the next item.

What will you write down on your list?
