# 7 Inspirational Quotes from BBCs Sherlock ...

There are dozens of memorable quotes from BBC’s Sherlock, even though there have only been six episodes. If you haven’t seen the show by now, what are you waiting for? Catch up before the next season returns (if that ever happens). Here are a few quotes from BBC’s Sherlock that can teach us lessons about life.

## 1. Helpful Friends

Sherlock says, “Alone is what I have. Alone protects me,” and John replies, “No. Friends protect people.” This exchange of dialogue shows two very different ways of thinking. Some believe that they’re better off alone, while others search for friends and a mate in order to make their life complete. Although people can cause you great pain, they can also cause you great happiness. Loved ones will be there for you and can make your life worth living. This is one of the quotes from BBC’s Sherlock that highlights the importance of friendship.

## 2. Changing Opinions

Sherlock says, “You've got a solution that you like, but you're choosing to ignore anything you see that doesn't comply with it.” When your mind believes the world is a certain way, it can be difficult to change your opinion. If you have a crush on someone, you ignore any mean things that they do. They’re perfect in your mind, so you can’t comprehend the fact that they’d do anything hurtful. This quote shows that you need to keep your mind open and view the world without bias. You can’t have one idea in your head that you refuse to alter, no matter what happens.

## 3. Living Life

Even though Lestrade says, “dont commit suicide” in a joking manner, it’s a serious matter. No one should ever harm themselves in any circumstance. If you loathe your life or yourself, talk to someone about it. Try to make your life better instead of ending it completely. No-one will be happy that you’re gone. You need to gather up your strength and try to make it through each passing day.

## 4. Pure Goodness

Sherlock says, “I may be on the side of the angels, but don't think for one second that I am one of them.” Everyone does bad things in life. No one can truly be considered an angel, because everyone has done things that they aren’t proud of. However, that doesn’t make them a bad person. You can do bad things and still be good.

## 5. Human Changeability

Moriarty says, “I am so changeable.” This is an important fact to keep in mind throughout life. Every person is fickle, to a certain extent. Someone might love you one day and leave you the next. They might be mean to you once, but act sweetly the next time you meet. Since humans have a wide range of emotions, we act differently at different times. Don’t expect someone to always stick to their word.

## 6. Staying in Place

Moriarty says, “Staying alive. It’s so boring, isn’t it? It’s just staying.” While you shouldn’t view life as boring, he makes a good point. People are so focused about constantly moving and being on the go. However, life is about staying alive. You don’t always need to be on your way somewhere. You can relax and enjoy the experience of staying in one place.

## 7. Possessing Power

Moriarty says, “In a world of locked rooms, the man with the key is king.” This quote can be a metaphor for the importance of knowledge. The man who has what no one else does is superior. If you want to succeed, you need to obtain the means to do so. You have the most power when you possess something that no one else has.

Go watch Sherlock to hear some more great lines! What’s your favorite quote from the show? Which character said it?
