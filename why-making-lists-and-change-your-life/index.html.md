# Why  Making Lists Can Change Your Life ...

Allow me to let you in to a little secret that you might like to know. Of all the most successful and productive people both in education and in the workplace, it is 99% likely that they are dedicated list makers! You might think that writing a to-do list Is just one more task to try to complete in a week or a day, but trust me, there is a reason why the practice is so popular! Here are seven solid reasons **why making lists can change your life** for the better!

## 1. Set Your Thoughts Free

Making lists of things that pop into your head is a great way of setting your thoughts free. There is a kind of therapy and enlightenment in having things written down; psychologically your mind feels lighter because you aren’t under as much pressure to remember every single thing.

## 2. More Success

The more to-do lists you have around the house, the more inspired you will be to continue to complete tasks to tick them off. This increase in productivity can only result in one thing - more success in all areas of your life.

## 3. Save Money

In particular, writing physical shopping lists can save you a lot of money. Going into a supermarket with a clear plan of action can help you to stay on budget compared to just browsing the aisles and picking up whatever takes your fancy at the time.

## 4. Self Esteem

The physical sight of seeing a to-do list where every single task has been completed can do wonders for your self-esteem. Even if they are little things that you have done, it is still an achievement, and seeing everything ticked off to give yourself a clean slate can feel really good.

## 5. No Mistakes

Writing out to-do lists of things that you need to do during a day or a week can ensure that you don’t make any big mistakes. You would be amazed at the amount of important tasks that can completely slip someone’s mind when they don’t have them written down.

## 6. Focus

Lists can help you to keep focused when you are having a busy or hectic day. Being able to refer back to a neat set of tasks when it might feel like you are rudderless can really help. It will keep you on track in terms of productivity and attention span.

## 7. Face up

Seeing tasks written down on paper will help you to finally face up to things that you have been putting off. Pushing it to the back of your mind is one thing, but seeing it there still unchecked on an otherwise completed list is something that should motivate you in to finally getting on with it!
