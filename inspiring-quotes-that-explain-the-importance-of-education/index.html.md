# 10 Inspiring Quotes That Explain the Importance of Education ...

When school gets tough and you wonder why you even spend hours on end studying, use these quotes that explain the importance of education. They will inspire you to uncover your thirst for knowledge. Getting a good education, whether in school or on your own, is the best decision you can make in your life. It can help you grow as a person and change the world. Although there are tons of quotes that explain the importance of education, these are a few of my personal favorites that keep me motivated through those late night study sessions and long textbook readings.

## 1. “Education is the Most Powerful Weapon Which You Can Use to Change the World.” – Nelson Mandela

If you ever expect to have an impact on the world, this quote from Nelson Mandela should be among the quotes that explain the importance of education. This quote should inspire you to want to learn about the world around you so you can change it. Subjects like history, science, math, and English can all lead you to learn the knowledge you will need to have an impact on the world.

## 2. “an Investment in Knowledge Pays the Best Interest.” – Benjamin Franklin

Especially with today’s increasing costs of college level education, this quote should speak volumes. While colleges can be incredibly expensive, it is one investment you can’t afford to not make. The opportunities you will be afforded in your college career and post-graduation will change your life forever. By getting a formal education, you will be opening so many doors for your future. The cost of college may seem high, but it is nothing in comparison to how much you would be getting back in return.

## 3. “Education is the Best Friend. an Educated Person is Respected Everywhere. Education Beats the Beauty and the Youth.” – Chanakya

Being intelligent is one of the most desirable qualities in a person. It trumps your looks, wealth, or age. Reading, learning, and a thirst for knowledge all lead you to become educated. Education is a universal currency and is respected in all corners of the world. Long after you grow up and your youthful looks leave you, you will always have your education.

## 4. “Education is Not the Filling of a Pail, but the Lighting of a Fire.” – William Butler Yeats

I personally feel that so many college students focus more on getting good grades than getting an actual education. GPA becomes more important than learning and retaining information, and that is such a shame. I miss the old days when school would inspire students to learn more and make a difference. This quote is one of my personal favorite quotes that explain the importance of education because it defines the purpose of education. It is not to get good grades and get a good job, it is to make you a better person with a thirst for more knowledge.

## 5. “Develop a Passion for Learning. if You do, You Will Never Cease to Grow.” – Anthony D’Angelo

Long after you go through high school and college, your education does not have to slow down. If you grow that appreciation and desire for education early on, nothing can hold it back. You should aspire to constantly be learning and growing, even after you have left school. Find a subject you are passionate about and learn everything there is to know. Read books, see art, and learn about the world. It will give you an appreciation for education and help you grow into an extraordinary person.

## 6. “Education is Not Preparation for Life; Education is Life Itself.” – John Dewey

All throughout college, they try to prepare us for life after graduation. They teach us how to interview, they edit our resumes, and they give us statistics about salary. But what about the focus on enjoying the learning process and growing as a person? This quote means so much to me because it reminds me that a job and success are great, but knowledge and education are so much more important. Life is not a 9 to 5 job, life is constantly growing and learning.

## 7. “I Would Rather Entertain and Hope That People Learned Something than Educate People and Hope They Were Entertained.” – Walt Disney

Education does not need to be sitting in a classroom and reading textbooks. Learning comes from all around us. This quote from Walt Disney is great because it is so evident in his work. He created an entire empire based on sharing happiness, and some of the greatest life lessons came from his work. I honestly believe that the lessons I learned from Disney have made me a better person today. You can become educated by enjoying life and being entertained, not just sitting in a lecture hall.

## 8. “the Direction in Which Education Starts a Man Will Determine His Future in Life.” – Plato

For all of those parents out there, instill a love for education early on and your child will have a great life. Education starts at a young age. The thirst to learn more and more about the world is a great thing when you're a child. That thirst will follow your child throughout his or her life and give them the desire to know everything they can.

## 9. “Education’s Purpose is to Replace an Empty Mind with an Open One." – Malcolm Forbes

One of the most important things about education is that it allows you to have an open mind. The more you know about the world, the more you can understand and respect it. Prejudice and stereotypes disappear if you can understand the world. Social issues become clearer when you are educated on the facts. Respect for the world is the best side effect of education.

## 10. “Education is Simply the Soul of a Society as It Passes from One Generation to Another.” – Gilbert Chesterton

It is not important for you to be educated for your own personal sake, but for the sake of those who come after you. Our collective knowledge has been built up for hundreds of years because those who are educated pass along their knowledge. Education should inspire you to share you knowledge with those who come after you. Take your inspiration and share it with your children to inspire them to become educated.

These quotes inspire me to not only successes in terms of grades, but actually take an active role in my education. What did you think of these quotes about the importance of education? What other quotes about education do you know of? Did these quotes inspire you to place a larger emphasis on the importance of education?
