# How I stopped overcomplicating success with John Davison Rockefeller quotes

Since he was such a successful figure, reading John Davison Rockefeller quotes provides a strong foundation for building motivation. Rockefeller lived from 1839 to 1937, during which time he became one of the world's richest men. In 1870, he built a refinery near Cleveland, Ohio, later incorporating it into the highly profitable Standard Oil Company. Standard Oil became so dominant that Rockefeller held a monopoly in the oil business, eventually leading to anti-monopoly laws like the Sherman Antitrust Act. While incredibly shrewd in business, he dedicated much of his later life to philanthropy, founding several institutions like [The University of Chicago](https://www.uchicago.edu/) and [The Rockefeller University](https://www.rockefeller.edu/). As you examine his life, it is no surprise that these opportunity motivational quotes offer valuable lessons today.

Here are a few quick takeaways you can expect to learn from his mindset:

- Finding opportunity in setbacks

- Prioritizing purpose over profit

- Building a lasting reputation

## 1. Go for Greatness

![Go for Greatness](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/x2/q2/vq/nmxx0mod_600x600.jpg)

One of my favorite statements from him is, “Don’t be afraid to give up the good to go for the great.” When examining the give up the good to go for the great meaning, I have found that it can sometimes be scary to set lofty goals and actually try to achieve them, especially if things are already going pretty well. The risk of failure when you are content with life often feels much more frightening. However, if you have a chance to make things even better, it makes perfect sense to take the leap. There have been several times in my own life where I have taken a giant step forward, and the rewards have been substantial. What I learned from those experiences is that it never hurts to attempt to achieve great things.

## 2. Opportunity

![Opportunity](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/cf/qy/ib/eq5nwf0q_600x600.jpg)

When something terrible in life happens, it can be easy to let that event bring you down. Yet, terrible events can often be viewed as a chance to improve your circumstances. Exploring the i always tried to turn every disaster into an opportunity meaning helps shift your perspective on setbacks. As Rockefeller stated in this famous every disaster is an opportunity quote, “I always tried to turn every disaster into an opportunity.” When I became injured and had to move in with my family, the adjustment was very difficult. Over time, however, I began to look at my situation as an opportunity to recover and connect with relatives I had not seen in quite some time. As terrible as the injury was, the hidden benefit was invaluable. As you go through life, try to remember that disasters often present new paths forward, much like the idea of [finding your passion](#4).

## 3. New Paths for Success

![New Paths for Success](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/ji/s4/qr/f91vzxth_600x372.jpg)

There are many versions of the idea of taking the road less traveled. Yet, I am particularly fond of this jd rockefeller quote: “If you want to succeed you should strike out on new paths, rather than travel the worn paths of accepted success.” If you look at some of the most successful people, you will find that they carved out their own lanes. Rockefeller is one classic example, but modern tech founders represent a more current model of the success that occurs through breaking out on your own. This doesn’t mean you have to invent the next great gadget. Rather, it might mean doing something different from your family's expectations, or finding a unique way to become a successful entrepreneur. Traveling down a different path can be exciting, and if you are successful, the rewards are immeasurable.

| Path Type | Potential Risk | Potential Reward |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Worn Path | Low risk | Standard results |
| New Path | High risk | Immeasurable success |

## 4. Money Isn’t Everything

![Money Isn’t Everything](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/vz/rg/cg/fcqmtz7q_600x665.jpg)

I am not entirely sure the statement, “If your only goal is to become rich, you will never achieve it,” holds true for everyone. However, what I do know is that money can only make a person so happy, while having a genuine passion for what you do brings lasting fulfillment and pushes you harder toward success. Not everyone is lucky enough to find their passion early on, but if you do have one and can mold it into a career, chances are you will achieve great things. You might have to be creative along the way, which connects directly back to [striking out on new paths](#3). To learn more about modern career passion, you can read advice from the [American Psychological Association](https://www.apa.org/topics/workplace).

## 5. Perseverance

![Perseverance](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/vr/jr/wd/sjdm9z3o_600x438.jpg)

The following words from Rockefeller are extremely profound: “I do not think that there is any other quality so essential to success of any kind as the quality of perseverance. It overcomes almost everything, even nature.” As a ballet dancer, I have watched many women achieve incredible milestones through pure perseverance, especially during my time at one of the top ballet schools in the country. It never ceased to amaze me to see someone execute a highly technical and difficult movement with pure grace and beauty. Having watched my own technical abilities improve through sheer determination, I always recognized that these beautiful performances were the direct result of perseverance. Obviously, this kind of dedication does not just apply to ballet or sports; it leads to positive outcomes in almost any area of your life, making it a wonderful quality to cultivate.

## 6. Character

![Character](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/np/xo/gl/pve8cuwb_403x275.jpg)

Rockefeller believed very strongly in maintaining a solid reputation and character, which is evident through his assertion, “The most important thing for a young man is to establish a credit…a reputation, character.” We can easily extend this advice to include anyone striving for success today. Having a reliable reputation can take you very far in life. If people trust and respect you, they are much more willing to help you reach your goals. I know I am always ready to support those I truly respect. I also tend to look up to people who act as positive role models and display strong moral character. Because I want to be a role model for others, I have worked diligently to be a trustworthy and reputable person.

## 7. Purpose

![Purpose](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/xi/jd/vb/e839mqam_420x294.jpg)

I mentioned earlier that having passion about what you do can take you quite far. Passion is not entirely different from having a true singleness of purpose, which Rockefeller considered essential for meaningful achievements. He emphasized that “singleness of purpose is one of the chief essentials for success in life, no matter what may be one’s aim.” Living with a singleness of purpose means leading a genuinely purpose-driven life. If you feel you have a clear purpose, everything you do takes on deeper meaning, which is critical not just for a successful career, but for a happier daily experience. Finding a purpose, whether it is big or small, will consistently keep you motivated. You can read more about living with intention through resources like [Greater Good Magazine](https://greatergood.berkeley.edu/article/item/how_to_find_your_purpose_in_life).

While some may consider his business practices hard-nosed, no one can deny that he reached the pinnacle of financial achievement. Many of these insights come directly from his practical experiences of how he built his empire. I find these john d rockefeller quotes to be highly encouraging, especially for anyone looking to build a lasting and rewarding career. Which of these pieces of advice did you find to be the most relevant to your own goals?
