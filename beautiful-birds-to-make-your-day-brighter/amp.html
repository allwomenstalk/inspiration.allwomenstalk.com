2 Orange and Blue

Via Pretties
This guy might be tiny, but his color gives him a big impact.

***

This vibrant image of a small bird with its striking colors of orange and blue is sure to brighten up anyone's day. The orange and blue colors of the bird's feathers contrast beautifully with each other, creating a stunning visual effect.

The bird in the image is a blue-crowned conure, also known as a sharp-tailed conure. It is a species of small parrot native to South America, with a range extending from Colombia and Venezuela south to Peru, Bolivia, and Brazil. The blue-crowned conure is the most common species of conure kept as a pet, and is known for its playful and affectionate nature.

The blue-crowned conure has a bright green body with a blue crown and forehead, and a bright orange-red tail. The underside of the bird is yellow or orange, and the wings are green with yellow at the base. The beak is black, and the eyes are dark brown.

These birds are found in tropical forests, woodlands, and savannas. They eat a variety of fruits, seeds, and nuts, and also feed on flowers and buds. They are social animals, and can often be seen in small flocks.

4 Itty Bitty

Via An image on imgfave
This tiny little green bird is so adorable! And he has such a great color.

***

The Itty Bitty bird is a small species of bird native to many parts of the world. It is a small, green bird with a short beak and a unique coloration. It is also known as the 'Green-breasted Bird' due to its vibrant green feathers.

The Itty Bitty is a social bird, often seen in flocks of up to 40 individuals. It feeds mainly on insects, but also consumes fruits, seeds, and some small vertebrates. It is an active bird, often seen flying around its habitat, searching for food.

The Itty Bitty is a very popular bird among birdwatchers. Its unique coloration and small size make it an ideal subject for photography. It is also a great subject for birders who are just starting out, as it is easy to spot and identify.

5 Hummingbird in Flight

Via Blue Black Scarf Check Scarf ...
Nothing beats seeing a hummingbird flying around. They are so much fun to watch.

***

Hummingbirds are the smallest of all birds, with some species weighing as little as 2 to 3 grams. They are found in the Americas, from Alaska to Tierra del Fuego, and have been observed in a variety of habitats, from tropical rainforest to desert. Hummingbirds are famous for their rapid wingbeat, which can reach up to 80 beats per second, allowing them to hover and fly backwards. They are also known for their long migrations, some species traveling up to 3,000 miles in a single journey. Hummingbirds feed on nectar and small insects, and have specially adapted beaks and tongues that help them extract food from flowers.

8 Quetzal

Via Blue Black Scarf Check Scarf ...
Those are some long tail feathers for such a tiny little bird.

***

The resplendent quetzal is truly captivating with its vibrant colors and astonishingly lengthy plumage. Originating from Central America, this small yet striking creature symbolizes freedom and wealth in many cultures and is even Guatemala's national bird. With a vivid green body, crimson breast and awe-inspiring tail feathers that can exceed two feet, the quetzal appears almost mythical. Spotting one in the cloud forests where they dwell is considered a lucky and unforgettable moment. Nature's artistry is epitomized in the elegant dance of the quetzal's tail feathers, weaving through the air as it flits from branch to branch.
